SkyRise Connecting Platform

Multi-tenant SaaS Solution • Multi-role and Multi-journey Platform

B2B digital ecosystem for the integral management of the helicopter leasing lifecycle, unifying Owners, Operators, and internal team in a single environment.

Based on: RFP “SkyRise Connecting Platform – Phase 0 MVP and Prototype” & Document “Initial Requirements and Process Description”.

What SkyRise Aims to Achieve

  • Unify fragmented operations into a scalable platform.
  • Automate manual processes (compliance, pricing).
  • Improve the experience for Owners and Operators.
  • Obtain real-time data visibility.

What Mindden Contributes

  • Consultative approach to mitigate risks in Phase 0.
  • Robust and agnostic Cloud-Native architecture.
  • Experience in complex B2B platforms.
  • Commitment to security and scalability NFRs from day one.

2. Understanding the Challenge and Actors

SkyRise needs to orchestrate the relationship between multiple actors in a high-value and regulated market.

Owner

Asset owners. Need visibility into the status and profitability of their helicopters.

Operator

Companies that operate the aircraft. Need agility in quotations and ease of compliance.

Company User

SkyRise operational staff who manage the day-to-day of contracts.

Support / Compliance / Admin

Cross-functional roles for technical support, regulatory validation, and system administration.

Scope of Journeys J1-J5:
  1. J1: Onboarding & Pricing
  2. J2: Search & Quote
  3. J3: Lease Management
  4. J4: Compliance & Reporting
  5. J5: Exit & Remarketing

Current Pain Points and Challenges

  • Company and asset onboarding is manual and fragmented.
  • There is a lack of a single, traceable view of the lease lifecycle.
  • Difficulty in consolidating operational and maintenance reports.
  • Overload in generating auditable compliance reports.

3. Platform Vision

The objective is to build the operating system for the leasing business, structured in clear functional modules.

The platform replaces manual processes and spreadsheets with guided and traceable workflows, reducing errors and increasing operational efficiency.

Business Value

  • Accelerate business growth through efficiency.
  • Ensure risk control and regulatory compliance.
  • Market differentiation through a superior digital experience.

Technological Value

  • Cloud-Native, scalable, and integrable architecture.
  • Secure platform with high observability (logging, metrics, tracing).
  • Easily governable and maintainable long-term.

Dashboards and KPIs per Role

The platform will offer personalized, real-time visibility, powered by the central data model:

Management

Aggregated view of the lease portfolio, financial profitability, risk exposure, and global business KPIs.

Operations

Status of active contracts, compliance alerts, pending validation tasks, and technical incidents.

Owners & Operators

Visibility of their assigned assets, quotation status, monthly reports, and contractual documentation.

Company and Asset Onboarding

Digital workflows for entity registration, KYC, and detailed helicopter inventory setup.

Search and Quotation

Advanced search engine and tools to generate fast and accurate quotations.

Lease Management

Complete contract lifecycle: signing, payment tracking, statuses, and alerts.

Compliance and Inspections

Management of maintenance reports, flight hours, and technical audits.

Financial and Analytical Module

Integration with invoicing and Business Intelligence dashboards.

5. MVP Functional Scope (Journeys J1-J5)

  • J1: Onboarding & Pricing

    Owner/Operator registration with validation. Technical registration of helicopters. Integration with data providers for pricing suggestion.

  • J2: Search & Quote

    Advanced search engine (technical/commercial filters). Helicopter detail page. Quotation request, offer issuance, and management of response SLAs.

  • J3: Lease Management

    Contract creation from the winning offer. Management of lease statuses. Control of payments, maintenance reserves, and contractual modifications.

  • J4: Compliance & Reporting

    Upload of monthly activity reports. Automatic data extraction (OCR) of hours and cycles. Discrepancy alerts and regulatory compliance.

  • J5: Exit & Remarketing

    Lease closure workflow. Consolidation of technical and financial information. Automatic asset preparation for remarketing.

    • MVP KPIs and Acceptance Criteria:
    • Complete functional coverage of the 5 critical journeys.
    • Adequate response times for critical operations.
    • Performance aligned with the NFRs defined in D6.
    • Sufficient AI precision level to support the operation, defined jointly with SkyRise.

7. Security, NFRs, and Observability

Security and Compliance

  • Network and Perimeter: WAF, DDoS protection, VPC with public/private subnets.
  • Admin Access: Use of Bastion Host and/or VPN to access internal environments.
  • Identity (IAM): OIDC/OAuth2, mandatory MFA, granular RBAC per role.
  • Encryption: TLS 1.3 (Transit), disk/DB encryption (Rest), centralized Secret Management.
  • Data: Retention, anonymization, and deletion policies aligned with data protection requirements.

Observability and NFRs

  • Key NFRs: Availability (Target SLA), Horizontal Scalability, Maintainability.
  • Performance: Performance aligned with the NFRs defined in D6.
  • Logging & Metrics: Centralized logging, Technical and business metrics.
  • Tracing: Distributed request tracing.
  • Operational Security: Integration with SIEM/SOC solutions.
  • Accessibility: Interface design following accessibility best practices (e.g., WCAG guidelines) to ensure inclusive use.

8. Data and Applied AI

Data Model

The model is structured to support transactions, analysis, and regulatory compliance.

Master Data

Entities (Owners/Operators), Assets (Helicopters), Users, Roles.

Transactional Data

Quotations, Contracts (Leases), Payments, Invoices.

Operational/Maintenance Data

Maintenance Reports, Flight Hours, Telemetry (time series), Logs.

AI Use Cases

Intelligent Pricing

Algorithms that cross-reference external market data to suggest competitive leasing prices.

Document OCR

Automated extraction of hours and cycles from PDF/image reports for J4.

Anomaly Detection

Pattern analysis in TimescaleDB to detect financial or operational inconsistencies.

pgVector and semantic search techniques will be used to locate relevant information in contractual documentation and logs, improving efficiency in complex queries and support.

9. Backlog and Quality

Traceability Matrix

SkyRise RFP ↔ Requirements ↔ Epics ↔ Stories ↔ Tests ↔ D1-D6

We ensure that every line of code responds to a documented business need.

Testing Strategy (DoR / DoD)

  • Test Types: Unit, API, UI, End-to-End, Security, Performance.
  • Definition of Ready (DoR): Clear Story, with ACs, estimated, and without blockers.
  • Definition of Done (DoD): Code reviewed, tests passed, documented, and deployed to QA.

Backlog Tool-Ready

The result of deliverable D4 is a backlog structured by epics and stories, ready to be imported into project management tools like Jira or Azure Boards. It includes tagging by journey (J1-J5), release, work type, and priorities, serving as the direct bridge for MVP execution.

11. Hosting Scenarios and Cost Scheme

Cost Scheme

  • Phase 0 Cost: Fixed Price. Billing by milestones: 40% Start / 40% Intermediate Delivery / 20% Closure.
  • MVP Development Cost: Estimation based on Master Story List (T&M or Fixed Price depending on risk).
  • Hosting Cost (OpEx): Recurring monthly, as an order of magnitude and dependent on the chosen Cloud scenario.

TCO will be optimized through the use of Reserved Instances and efficient auto-scaling, maintaining the Cloud-Native architecture.

Amazon Web Services (AWS)

  • Compute: Amazon EKS (Managed Kubernetes).
  • Database: Amazon RDS for PostgreSQL. TimescaleDB self-managed or Cloud.
  • Storage: Amazon S3 (Standard + Infrequent Access).
  • Advantage: Maturity in data and analytics services.

Microsoft Azure

  • Compute: Azure Kubernetes Service (AKS).
  • Database: Azure Database for PostgreSQL (Flexible Server).
  • Storage: Azure Blob Storage.
  • Advantage: Native integration with the Microsoft corporate ecosystem.

12. Team, Governance, and Risks

Phase 0 Team

  • Delivery / Project Manager: Commitment and risk management.
  • Solution Architect: Technical definition and D6.
  • Business Analyst / Proxy PO: Functional definition D1, D3, D4.
  • UX/UI Designer: Prototyping D5.
  • Tech Leads (FE/BE): Support in estimation and feasibility.
  • DevOps / QA: CI/CD and Quality Strategy.

Project Governance Model

The project will be governed by a **SkyRise–Mindden Project Committee** responsible for scope, timeline, and risks.

  • Cadence: Regular follow-up meetings (weekly/bi-weekly).
  • Reviews: Bi-weekly progress reviews on D1–D6 deliverables and UX prototype.
  • Tools: Use of Jira/Azure Boards (Backlog), Confluence/SharePoint (Documentation), and Figma/Miro (UX Design).

Assumptions and Exclusions

  • Assumptions: Active participation of SkyRise personnel for workshops. Access to documentation and sample data. Basic hosting infrastructure available.
  • Exclusions: Functional code development in Phase 0. Deployment to production (except for demo environments). Third-party licenses.

Risk Matrix

  • Stakeholder Availability: Risk of validation delays. Mitigation: Pre-approved session calendar.
  • Legacy Data Quality: Complexity in migration. Mitigation: Early analysis in D1.
  • External Dependencies: Third-party integrations (Pricing). Mitigation: Early definition of interface contracts.
  • Scope/Priority Changes: MVP deviation. Mitigation: Strict change control process and backlog management.
